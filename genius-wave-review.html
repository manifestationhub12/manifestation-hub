Genius Wave is one of the most popular manifestation audio programs online. But does it actually work, or is it just clever marketing? Here's my honest, detailed review.

What Is Genius Wave?

Genius Wave is a 7-minute audio track designed to be listened to daily. It uses specific sound frequencies — inspired by theta brainwave research — intended to quiet the analytical mind and create a receptive state for reprogramming subconscious beliefs around wealth and abundance.

The core idea: theta brainwaves (4–8 Hz) are the state your brain enters during deep meditation, hypnosis, and the moments just before sleep. This is the state where deep-seated beliefs are most accessible. Genius Wave aims to guide your brain into this state using audio.

How It Works

You simply listen to the audio for 7 minutes a day, ideally with headphones, in a quiet space. Many users listen in the morning or before bed. There's no active effort required — no writing, no visualization, no ritual. Just listen.

The track uses layered frequencies (binaural-style tones) that are said to help entrain the brain into a theta state. Whether the science is fully proven or not, the experience is genuinely relaxing — and relaxation alone has documented benefits for mindset.

Important context: there is genuine research on theta brainwaves and their role in memory, creativity, and belief formation. However, the specific claims made by Genius Wave go beyond what published studies confirm. Treat it as a helpful practice, not a medical device.

I used Genius Wave every night for about three weeks as part of a sleep routine. Here's what I noticed: the audio is genuinely pleasant and helped me fall asleep faster. By week two, I felt calmer during the day and less reactive to financial stress.

Did money magically appear? No. But my mindset shifted — I became more aware of opportunities, less anxious about spending, and more confident in my decisions. For me, that shift alone was worth it.
